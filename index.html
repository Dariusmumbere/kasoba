About

Rooted in Kasese,
reaching far beyond it.

Kasoba Godwin is a Ugandan filmmaker, producer, director, screenwriter and actor whose work reflects a deep commitment to authentic storytelling and social impact. Over nine years and eleven completed projects, he's built a portfolio that moves across roles rather than settling into one — directing one year, producing the next, stepping in front of the camera when the story calls for it.

He founded SaintHood Film Production in 2017, a Kasese-based company dedicated to developing compelling stories and nurturing local talent in Western Uganda's growing film scene. His storytelling stays close to real life — resilience, social challenges, love and human transformation — and it has carried him to recognition in Uganda, the United Kingdom and beyond.

But the camera is only one of his tools. Kasoba lives within the FAPCAN community in Kasese, where caring for orphans, widows, elderly and disabled neighbours is as much a part of his daily practice as writing a script — two ways of doing the same work: paying attention to people who are easy to overlook.

Featured Work

Three recent projects, all shot in the foothills of the Rwenzori — about land, dignity, and the people who work both.

2026

Seeds of Rwenzori

Director / Producer

A journey into Kasese District, where the Bakonzo community plants beans and maize in rich black soil beneath the Rwenzori Mountains. The film follows the work of a planting season — and the songs, dances and shared meals that surround it.

2025

Beans Our Food

Director / Producer

In the foothills of the Rwenzori, beans are more than a crop — they pay school fees and steady whole households. This documentary follows the farmers who plant, harvest, dry and clean them by hand, season after season.

2026

The Blind Boy

Cinematographer / Director

A blind boy is struck and abandoned by a careless rider on a quiet road. When that same rider later breaks down nearby, it's the boy he wronged who returns a missing part — a short, quiet story about grace finding its way back.

Kasoba lives within the FAPCAN community in Kasese, where caring for people who are easy to overlook is daily work, not an outreach campaign. He sees it as the same instinct that drives his films — paying close attention to lives that don't usually make it to a screen.
